    My journey into cybersecurity began unexpectedly with a double major in Criminology and Sociology. Fascinated by human behavior, I became captivated by the social engineering attack on Twitter used to promote cryptocurrency scams. The attack exploited human tendencies, such as confirmation bias and the predictable responses employees make when trusting their colleagues. A lack of training made employees vulnerable, and the attackers first targeted lower-level employees before escalating to more privileged accounts. This incident reinforced that cybersecurity isn’t just about technology—it’s about understanding how human behavior contributes to vulnerabilities - a concept that relates very heavily to what I studied for my undergraduate degree.

    Today, I specialize in digital forensics, network security, and user authentication, using my investigative mindset to build resilient security measures. I’m passionate about proactively defending organizations and contributing to a safer digital world.
